Audition

Hey there! Birdstrike Theatre is always needing new, awesome people to do improv with. Auditions take a lot of time, though, and we like to focus on team building throughout the year, so we audition once a year in fall quarter. If you can't make the auditions, sorry, you'll have to audition the following year.

For this year's audition days and times, see the schedule.

The Process

We generally have two days for auditions, but just come to one. We split it up into two days to help ensure people can make at least one day as well as to give everyone more stage time. Each audition session is usually 2 hours.

We also like to spend some time getting to know people a little bit better outside of improvisation, so we'll ask you to sign up for a quick 15 minute interview around the time of auditions. If you haven't spoken with us during our MU tabling or at the workshop to set up an interview time please email us and we'll set up a time.

The people from auditions we need to see more of to make a decision will be invited to callbacks, which occurs soon after auditions. This gives you another opportunity to bring your A-game if you feel you didn't do your best during your first audition.

Unfortunately we don't have room to let everyone who auditions into Birdstrike. There are many wonderful, talented, awesome people we utterly love who just don't make it in for whatever reason. Don't be discouraged- get involved in other improv groups and watch Birdstrike shows to see how we work together. We encourage you to audition the following year, as a number of us have gotten in the second time around!

Summary

Here's what you'll need to do to audition:

  1. Interview: a quick, informal interview so we can get to know you a little better. Set a time at the workshop or by emailing us.
  2. Audition: come to 1 of the two audition times.
  3. Callbacks: some people we need to see a bit more of to make a decision. Callbacks will occur later on the same day as the last day of auditions.

What We Look For

First off, no prior improv experience is necessary. We look for people who are fun, playful and creative. People who support their scene partners and make them look good. We do long form improv, so we're not necessarily looking for quick, jokey players, but rather people who can embrace characters and do deeper, situational humor.

If you haven't done improv before don't be scared, just come, have fun and play with us!
